In Quantitative Literacy, you will learn how to read, understand, and interpret the numerical data that permeates our lives. You will apply problem-solving skills to make financial decisions, use proportional reasoning to convert units, apply your knowledge of percentages to multistage processes, apply the appropriate amount of precision in making claims, recognize the difference between linear and exponential growth, and use two-way tables to analyze false negatives and false positives. You will become aware of how mathematics may be used by others to mislead you and how to ensure that you are interpreting and presenting your results honestly and accurately.
