We solve the equation \(2P=P1.06^{t}\text{.}\) We can divide both sides by \(P\text{,}\) leaving \(2=1.06^{t}\text{.}\) One way to complete this problem is to graph \(y=1.06^x\) and \(y=2\) on the same coordinate graph (using technology) to see where the curves intersect. To solve this by computations, we would take a logarithm (log or ln) of both sides to get \(t=\frac{log(2)}{log(1.06)}\approx11.9\text{.}\)
