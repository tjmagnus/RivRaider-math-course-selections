    Chapter 5 MA127 Geometrical Explorations

    MA127 is an exploratory, tactile course in which students observe geometric relationships, make conjectures, and test them. Topics include construction and measurement of 2-dimensional and 3-dimensional figures, tilings, transformations, regularity, congruence and similarity, and impossibility. Students practice thinking outside the box, exploring why geometrical formulas work, and making arguments that use diagrams and words. The topics are chosen to help Education majors prepare for the Praxis and similar licensure exams, but students of all majors are welcome to develop their tactile, visual, and verbal reasoning skills, to strengthen their geometrical understanding, and to make clear logic arguments. If you like building things and making art, this might be a good fit for you.

    Section 5.1 What will I explore in MA127?

    Topics covered may vary, but often includes the following:
    • Determine the formula for the area of a trapezoid by cutting it up into rectangles, triangles, and/or parallelograms.
    • Explain why the Pythagorean theorem holds by rearranging triangles and squares into other shapes.
    • Create tilings and quilt patterns and discover which regular polygons can be used to make symmetric designs.
    • Build three-dimensional solids with polygonal faces and explore the possibilities and patterns.
    • Determine which logos have reflective and rotational symmetry and discover the relationships between the angle of rotation and the number of reflecting lines.
    • Discover what happens when you reflect a figure across two lines in succession. When is it a rotation? A translation?
    • What number patterns arise from the study of star polygons, kaleidoscopes, billiard ball paths, and fractals?
    • What happens to familiar geometrical properties when you create shapes on a sphere instead of a flat surface? Do they still hold? Are there other geometries other that Euclidean geometry?

    Section 5.2 Am I ready for MA127?

    To be successful in MA127, you need to be able to do basic algebra including operations on fractions, working with exponents and square roots, plotting points on a graph, simplifying expressions, and solving equations. You should be able to follow the steps in a task and think outside the box. Good writing skills will help as you learn how to develop clear explanations and incorporate visuals and symbolic expressions. Some fundamental topics such as computing areas and perimeters, using the Pythagorean Theorem, and the simplifying square roots will be reviewed briefly at the appropriate time in the course. Most importantly, you are expected to approach mathematical tasks with a "can-do" attitude, a sense of curiosity, and a willingness to explore ideas with others, to notice patterns, and to give explanations.

    Section 5.3 Next Steps

    If you are ready to sign up for MA127 Geometrical Explorations or have decided on another mathematics course, you may return to the Directed Self-Placement Tools and record your selection. If you would like to explore other options, for completing your mathematics requirement, you may look at MA123 Quantitative Literacy, MA126 Puzzles, Patterns, and Probabilities, or MA130 Functions. If you are concerned about your readiness for these mathematics courses, you may explore MA100 Math Refresher as an option before making your decision.
