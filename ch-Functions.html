    Chapter 7 MA130 Functions

    MA130 gives students the preparation they need to succeed in calculus and to apply their knowledge of algebraic and transcendental functions to other fields of study. Linear, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric, polynomial, rational, and piecewise-defined functions are examined from symbolic, graphical, numerical, and verbal points of view. Topics include¬†sketching and analyzing graphs, rates of change, inverses, and transformations of functions. Applications to science, social science, and business will be included. Students will be encouraged to become more independent mathematical thinkers as they solve problems.
    This course is not available for credit to students who have successfully completed a collegiate course equivalent to MA 165 or higher. On the other hand, students who took a calculus course in high school which focused primarily on computations with little attention to theory and applications may benefit from MA130.
    The prerequisites for MA130 are the same as the learning outcomes for MA112. It will be assumed that students can simplify algebraic expressions, solve linear and quadratic equations, work with exponents and radicals, compute the slope and give an equation of a line through two points, and work with functional notation. Sample prerequisite problems may be viewed at Chapter¬†3 and will help you gage your readiness for MA130. If most of these algebra problems in Chapter¬†3 are difficult for you, you might want to begin in MA112 instead of MA130.

    Exercises What will I learn in MA130 Functions

    At the end of MA130, you should be able to do the following. You do not need to be able to do these problems at the beginning of the course. Trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions will be introduced in MA130, but students should already have a strong grasp of linear functions and the ability to simplify expressions involving polynomials, radicals, and exponents.

    1. Compute and Analyze Rates of Change.

    1. Given \(f(x)=x^2-3x\text{,}\) determine the rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \(x=1\) to \(x=4\text{.}\)
    2. Identify from the graph of \(y=g(x)\text{,}\) given below, where \(g(x)\) has a positive rate of change.
    Answer.
    a. \(2\text{;}\) (b) Function \(g\) has a positive rate of change on the interval \(-2\lt x \lt 2\text{.}\)

    2. Work with functions defined graphically.

    Given the graph of \(y=g(x)\) above, determine the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle y=g(3)\)
    2. The solution(s) of \(g(x)=2\text{.}\)

    3. Work with functions defined verbally.

    The money in a bank account is increasing by 5% every year. There is $30,000 in the account in 2020.
    1. Write a formula for $A(t)$, the amount of money in the account \(t\) years after 2020.
    2. Determine the amount of money in the account in 2025.
    3. In what year will the amount of money reach $100,000?
    Hint.
    Is this function linear, exponential, or something else?
    Answer.
    (a) \(A(t)=30000(1.05)^t\text{;}\) (b) $38,288 exactly 5 years after it had $30,000; (c) During late 2044 or approximately 25 years after 2020
    Solution.
    (a) The growth is exponential. Each year we have the amount that was in the account the previous year plus an additional 0.05 times that amount. Thus, we are multiplying the amount in the account by $1+0.05$ each year giving a formula of \(A(t)=30000(1.05)^t\text{.}\) (b) \(30000(1.05)^5\approx 32.288\text{;}\) (c) Solve \(100000=30000(1.05)^t\) using logarithms or by viewing where the curves of \(y=100000\) and \(y=30000(1.05)^t\) intersect on a graphing calculator or similar app. To use logarithms,
    \begin{align*} \frac{10}{3}\amp=1.05^t \\ ln\left(\frac{10}{3}\right)\amp=t(ln(1.05))\\ \frac{ln(\frac{10}{3})}{ln(1.05)}\amp=t\\ t\approx 24.68 \end{align*}

    4. Determine the inverse of a function and use it to solve problems.

    1. Determine the inverses of the following functions:
      1. \(\displaystyle f(x)=3x-7\)
      2. \(\displaystyle g(x)=\frac{5}{x+8}\)
      3. \(\displaystyle h(t)=2\ln(6t)\)
    2. Use inverse properties to simplify \(f^{-1}(f(3y))-y\text{.}\)
    3. Determine the angle \(\theta\) such that \(\tan\theta=-1\) and \(0\lt\theta\lt \pi\text{.}\)

    5. Combine and transform functions.

    1. Let \(f(x)=x+3\) and \(g(x)=2x^2-x+4\text{.}\) Determine the following:
      1. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)\)
      2. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)\)
      3. \(\displaystyle g\circ f(x)\)
    2. Describe how the graph of \(h(x)=-2f(x)\) is related to the graph of \(y=f(x)\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    a. i. \((f+g)(x)=2x^2+7\text{;}\) ii. \((fg)(2)=50\text{;}\) iii. \(f\circ g(x)=2x^2+11x+19\) b. The graph is reflected over the \(x\)-axis and stretched vertically by a factor of 2.
    Solution.
      1. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)=(x+3)+(2x^2-x+4)=2x^2+7\)
      2. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)=f(2)g(2)=5\cdot 10=50\)
      3. \(\displaystyle f\circ g(x)=2(x+3)^2-(x+3)+4 =2x^2+12x+18-x-3+4=2x^2+11x+19\)

    6. Recognize families of functions by their graphs and develop a formula for a graphed function.

    Describe each pictured graph as linear, polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, absolute value, or sinusoidal. Give a formula for the function based on the function family and key points.
    Answer.
    (a) \(f(x)=2-|x-4|\text{;}\) (b) \(f(t)=2cos(t)+1\)
    MA130 is the right course for you if you are strong in algebra techniques (see Chapter¬†3 for comparison) but find some or all of the above problems to be new or difficult. In MA130, there will also be an emphasis on doing problems that are closer to how you encounter mathematics beyond the classroom; your ability to take a mathematical situation and rephrase it into a formula will grow. On the other hand, if you are comfortable completing most of the above problems on your own with only minor errors, then you may be ready to enroll in MA165 Calculus or a higher-level course. Talk to your advisor and/or the mathematics program director to help you determine the right course for you. Credit for Advanced Placement exams will be considered once scores are received.
