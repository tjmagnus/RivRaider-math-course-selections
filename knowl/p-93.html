The content of MA126 may vary, but some of the questions below are often explored:
  • If I could fold a paper in half 50 times, how high would it be?
  • Continue the sequence \(3,4,7,11,18,\dots\text{.}\) What do we notice about the ratios \(\frac{4}{3},\frac{7}{4}, \frac{11}{7},\frac{18}{11},\dots\text{?}\)
  • Why must there be an infinite number of primes? What role do prime numbers play in our digital world?
  • Is \(\frac{\sqrt{56}}{\sqrt{14}}\) a rational number? How do I write \(23.154154\dots\) as a fraction?
  • What is the missing digit in the UPC code \(02481000551\)X?
  • Whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, real numbers: Are these sets of numbers the same size or are there different sizes of infinity?
  • What is the probability that two people in the class share a birthday?
  • How can I use logic and mathematics to ensure that I will win a two-player game? Does it matter whether I play first or second?
