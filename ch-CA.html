    Chapter 3 MA112 College Algebra

    MA112 College Algebra reviews and strengthens a student‚Äôs algebraic skills in preparation for MA130 Functions. Although it counts as the Rivier University general education mathematics core requirement, the Mathematics Program does not recommend MA112 as a final mathematics course. In addition, MA112 is typically not accepted for transfer at other colleges. You should only enroll in MA112 if you are considering taking MA130 Functions in the future.
    Topics include solving linear and polynomial equations and inequalities, simplifying and evaluating polynomial, rational, radical, piecewise-defined, exponential and logarithmic expressions, graphing linear and quadratic functions, and solving applied problems and systems of linear equations.

    Exercises What will I learn in MA112 College Algebra

    At the end of MA112, you should be able to do the following:

    1. Simplify Algebraic Expressions.

    Simplify the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2-5(x+4)^2\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \frac{6x^2-6}{2x^2-4x+4}\)
    Answer.
    a. \(-2x^2-40x-80\text{;}\) (b) \(\frac{3(x+1)}{x-1}\)

    2. Solve Linear and Quadratic Equations.

    Solve each of the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle 4x-\frac{2}{3}(x+3)=\frac{1}{3}(x-12)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (x-4)(x+5)=36\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(x=-2\text{;}\) (b) \(x=7\) or \(x=-8\)

    3. Work with exponents and radicals.

    Simplify the following expressions:
    1. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{2x}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{9y^2}{14x^5}\right)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{9x^2 y^6}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{24x^8}{15x^3}\right)^{-2}\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(\frac{4y^2}{21x^2}\text{;}\) (b) \(3xy^3\text{;}\) (c) \(\frac{25}{64x^{10}}\)

    4. Determine an Equation for a Line.

    Determine an equation for the line that passes through the point \((10,4)\) and is perpendicular to the line \(y=5x-12\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    Possible solutions include \(y=-\frac{1}{5}x+6\) and \(x+5y=30\text{.}\)
    Solution.
    In order for the line to be perpendicular to \(y=5x-12\text{,}\) the slope of the line must be \(-\frac{1}{5}\text{.}\) We can then use this slope together with the point \((10,4)\) to find the \(y\)-intercept by solving \(4=-\frac{1}{5}(10)+b\text{.}\) Another option is to substitute into the point-slope form to get, \(y-4=-\frac{1}{5}(x-10)\text{.}\)

    5. Solve systems of linear equations.

    Find the ordered pair that satisfies the system:
    \begin{align*} x+2y&=25\\ 10x+15y&=210 \end{align*}
    Answer.
    \((x,y)=(9,8)\text{;}\) that is, \(x=9\) and \(y=8\text{.}\)

    6. Factor polynomials and solve polynomial equations.

    1. Factor \(28x^3y^5-14x^5y^2\text{.}\)
    2. Solve \(81x^4=16\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(14x^3y^2(2y^3-x^2)\text{;}\) (b) \(x=\pm\frac{2}{3}\)

    7. Combine and evaluate functions.

    Let \(f(x)=x+3\) and \(g(x)=2x^2-x+4\text{.}\) Determine the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle g(10)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle g\circ f(x)\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(g(10)=194\text{;}\) (b) \((f+g)(x)=2x^2+7\text{;}\) (c) \((fg)(2)=50\text{;}\) (d) \(f\circ g(x)==2x^2+11x+19\)
    Solution.
    1. \(\displaystyle g(10)=2(10)^2-10+4=200-10+4=194\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)=(x+3)+(2x^2-x+4)=2x^2+7\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)=f(2)g(2)=5\cdot 10=50\)
    4. \(\displaystyle f\circ g(x)=2(x+3)^2-(x+3)+4 =2x^2+12x+18-x-3+4=2x^2+11x+19\)
    If you feel comfortable completing most of the above problems on your own with only minor errors, then you should be ready to enroll in MA130 Functions which is described in Chapter¬†7. If you had difficulty even starting most of the problems, you might want to look at MA100 Math Refresher, Chapter¬†2 to strengthen the mathematical skills needed for MA112 College Algebra and other courses. If you are able to do most of the problems in Chapter¬†2 but not many of the problems on this page, MA112 College Algebra is the right course for you.
    If you are ready to confirm your mathematics course selection, return to the Directed Self-Placement and check the box for the desired course.
