    Chapter 5 MA130 Functions

    MA130 gives students the preparation they need to succeed in calculus and to apply their knowledge of algebraic and transcendental functions to other fields of study. Linear, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric, polynomial, rational, and piecewise-defined functions are examined from symbolic, graphical, numerical, and verbal points of view.

    Section 5.1 Topics in MA130 Functions

    Topics include¬†sketching and analyzing graphs, rates of change, inverses, and transformations of functions. Applications to science, social science, and business will be included. Students will be encouraged to become more independent mathematical thinkers as they solve problems.
    This course is not available for credit to students who have successfully completed a collegiate course equivalent to MA 165 or higher. On the other hand, students who took a calculus course in high school which focused primarily on computations with little attention to theory and applications may benefit from MA130.
    The prerequisites for MA130 are the same as the learning outcomes for MA112 College Algebra, a course recommended only for students who plan also to take MA130 Functions. It will be assumed that students can simplify algebraic expressions, solve linear and quadratic equations, work with exponents and radicals, compute the slope and give an equation of a line through two points, and work with functional notation.

    Exercises What will I learn in MA130 Functions?

    At the end of MA130, you should be able to do the following. You do not need to be able to do these problems at the beginning of the course. Trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions will be introduced in MA130, but students should already have a strong grasp of linear functions and the ability to simplify expressions involving polynomials, radicals, and exponents.
    1. Compute and Analyze Rates of Change.
    1. Given \(f(x)=x^2-3x\text{,}\) determine the rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \(x=1\) to \(x=4\text{.}\)
    2. Identify from the graph of \(y=g(x)\text{,}\) given below, where \(g(x)\) has a positive rate of change.
    Answer.
    a. \(2\text{;}\) (b) Function \(g\) has a positive rate of change on the interval \(-2\lt x \lt 2\text{.}\)
    2. Work with functions defined graphically.
    Given the graph of \(y=g(x)\) above, determine the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle y=g(3)\)
    2. The solution(s) of \(g(x)=2\text{.}\)
    3. Work with functions defined verbally.
    The money in a bank account is increasing by 5% every year. There is $30,000 in the account in 2020.
    1. Write a formula for $A(t)$, the amount of money in the account \(t\) years after 2020.
    2. Determine the amount of money in the account in 2025.
    3. In what year will the amount of money reach $100,000?
    Hint.
    Is this function linear, exponential, or something else?
    Answer.
    (a) \(A(t)=30000(1.05)^t\text{;}\) (b) $38,288 exactly 5 years after it had $30,000; (c) During late 2044 or approximately 25 years after 2020
    Solution.
    (a) The growth is exponential. Each year we have the amount that was in the account the previous year plus an additional 0.05 times that amount. Thus, we are multiplying the amount in the account by $1+0.05$ each year giving a formula of \(A(t)=30000(1.05)^t\text{.}\) (b) \(30000(1.05)^5\approx 32.288\text{;}\) (c) Solve \(100000=30000(1.05)^t\) using logarithms or by viewing where the curves of \(y=100000\) and \(y=30000(1.05)^t\) intersect on a graphing calculator or similar app. To use logarithms,
    \begin{align*} \frac{10}{3}\amp=1.05^t \\ ln\left(\frac{10}{3}\right)\amp=t(ln(1.05))\\ \frac{ln(\frac{10}{3})}{ln(1.05)}\amp=t\\ t\approx 24.68 \end{align*}
    4. Determine the inverse of a function and use it to solve problems.
    1. Determine the inverses of the following functions:
      1. \(\displaystyle f(x)=3x-7\)
      2. \(\displaystyle g(x)=\frac{5}{x+8}\)
      3. \(\displaystyle h(t)=2\ln(6t)\)
    2. Use inverse properties to simplify \(f^{-1}(f(3y))-y\text{.}\)
    3. Determine the angle \(\theta\) such that \(\tan\theta=-1\) and \(0\lt\theta\lt \pi\text{.}\)
    5. Combine and transform functions.
    1. Let \(f(x)=x+3\) and \(g(x)=2x^2-x+4\text{.}\) Determine the following:
      1. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)\)
      2. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)\)
      3. \(\displaystyle g\circ f(x)\)
    2. Describe how the graph of \(h(x)=-2f(x)\) is related to the graph of \(y=f(x)\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    a. i. \((f+g)(x)=2x^2+7\text{;}\) ii. \((fg)(2)=50\text{;}\) iii. \(f\circ g(x)=2x^2+11x+19\) b. The graph is reflected over the \(x\)-axis and stretched vertically by a factor of 2.
    Solution.
      1. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)=(x+3)+(2x^2-x+4)=2x^2+7\)
      2. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)=f(2)g(2)=5\cdot 10=50\)
      3. \(\displaystyle f\circ g(x)=2(x+3)^2-(x+3)+4 =2x^2+12x+18-x-3+4=2x^2+11x+19\)
    6. Recognize families of functions by their graphs and develop a formula for a graphed function.
    Describe each pictured graph as linear, polynomial, exponential, logarithmic, absolute value, or sinusoidal. Give a formula for the function based on the function family and key points.
    Answer.
    (a) \(f(x)=2-|x-4|\text{;}\) (b) \(f(t)=2cos(t)+1\)

    Section 5.2 Are you ready for MA130?

    Some students need to reinforce their algebraic skills and understanding to prepare for success in MA130. If you are able to do most of the problems in the next set on your own, then you should do well in MA130. If your degree and career plan includes a need for Functions or Calculus and you struggle with most of these prerequisite problems, then you may want to start in MA112. If you do not plan on taking MA130 later, we recommend that you choose one of the other options accessible from the left sidebar.

    Exercises What will I learn in MA112 College Algebra

    At the end of MA112, you should be able to do the following:
    1. Simplify Algebraic Expressions.
    Simplify the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2-5(x+4)^2\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \frac{6x^2-6}{2x^2-4x+2}\)
    Answer.
    a. \(-2x^2-40x-80\text{;}\) (b) \(\frac{3(x+1)}{x-1}\)
    Solution.
    1. \(\displaystyle 3x^2-5(x+4)^2=3x^2-5(x+4)(x+4)=3x^2-5x^2-40x-80\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \frac{6x^2-6}{2x^2-4x+2}= \frac{6(x+1)(x-1)}{2(x-1)(x-1)}=\frac{3(x+1)}{x-1}\)
    2. Solve Linear and Quadratic Equations.
    Solve each of the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle 4x-\frac{2}{3}(x+3)=\frac{1}{3}(x+12)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (x-4)(x+5)=36\)
    Hint.
    Using a guess-and-check method does not qualify as understanding this concept.
    Answer.
    (a) \(x=2\text{;}\) (b) \(x=7\) or \(x=-8\)
    Solution.
    1. One method of solving this problem is to use the distributive property to get \(4x-\frac{2x}{3}-2=\frac{x}{3}+4\text{.}\) Adding \(2-\frac{x}{3}\) to both sides gives \(4x-\frac{2x}{3}-\frac{x}{3}=4+2\) or \(4x-x=6\text{.}\) Thus, \(3x=6\) and \(x=2\text{.}\)
    2. Expanding the left side, we get \(x^2+x-20=36\) which can be rewritten as \(x^2+x-56=0\text{.}\) Factoring the polynomial gives us \((x-7)(x+8)=0\) so that \(x=7\) or \(x=-8\text{.}\)
    3. Work with exponents and radicals.
    Simplify the following expressions:
    1. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{9x^2 y^6}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{24x^8}{15x^3}\right)^{-2}\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(3xy^3\text{;}\) (b) \(\frac{4y^2}{21x^2}\text{;}\) (c) \(\frac{25}{64x^{10}}\)
    Solution.
    1. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{9x^2 y^6}=\sqrt{9}\sqrt{x^2}\sqrt{y^6}=3xy^3\)
    2. \begin{align*} \left(\frac{2x}{3}\right)^3 \left(\frac{9y^2}{14x^5}\right) \amp=\left(\frac{(2x)^3}{3^3}\right) \left(\frac{9y^2}{14x^5}\right)\\ \amp=\frac{8x^3\cdot 9y^2}{27\cdot 14x^5} =\frac{4y^2}{21x^2} \end{align*}
    3. \(\displaystyle \left(\frac{24x^8}{15x^3}\right)^{-2} =\left(\frac{8x^5}{5}\right)^{-2}=\left(\frac{64x^{10}}{25}\right)^{-1} =\frac{25}{64x^{10}}\)
    4. Determine an Equation for a Line.
    Determine an equation for the line that passes through the point \((10,4)\) and is perpendicular to the line \(y=5x-12\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    Possible solutions include \(y=-\frac{1}{5}x+6\) and \(x+5y=30\text{.}\)
    Solution.
    In order for the line to be perpendicular to \(y=5x-12\text{,}\) the slope of the line must be \(-\frac{1}{5}\text{.}\) We can then use this slope together with the point \((10,4)\) to find the \(y\)-intercept by solving \(4=-\frac{1}{5}(10)+b\text{.}\) Another option is to substitute into the point-slope form to get, \(y-4=-\frac{1}{5}(x-10)\text{.}\)
    5. Solve systems of linear equations.
    Find the ordered pair that satisfies the system:
    \begin{align*} x+2y&=25\\ 10x+15y&=210 \end{align*}
    Hint.
    You should be able to solve this problem algebraically either by elimination or by the substitution method. Using a guess-and-check strategy does not count as understanding this concept.
    Answer.
    \((x,y)=(9,8)\text{;}\) that is, \(x=9\) and \(y=8\text{.}\)
    6. Factor polynomials and solve polynomial equations.
    1. Factor \(28x^3y^5-14x^5y^2\text{.}\)
    2. Solve \(81x^4=16\text{.}\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(14x^3y^2(2y^3-x^2)\text{;}\) (b) \(x=\pm\frac{2}{3}\)
    7. Combine and evaluate functions.
    Let \(f(x)=x+3\) and \(g(x)=2x^2-x+4\text{.}\) Determine the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle g(10)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)\)
    4. \(\displaystyle g\circ f(x)\)
    Answer.
    (a) \(g(10)=194\text{;}\) (b) \((f+g)(x)=2x^2+7\text{;}\) (c) \((fg)(2)=50\text{;}\) (d) \(f\circ g(x)=2x^2+11x+19\)
    Solution.
    1. \(\displaystyle g(10)=2(10)^2-10+4=200-10+4=194\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)=(x+3)+(2x^2-x+4)=2x^2+7\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (fg)(2)=f(2)g(2)=5\cdot 10=50\)
    4. \begin{align*} f\circ g(x)\amp=2(x+3)^2-(x+3)+4\\ \amp=2x^2+12x+18-x-3+4=2x^2+11x+19 \end{align*}

    Section 5.3 Choosing Your Course

    MA130 is the right course for you if you can do most of the algebraic techniques in Section¬†5.2, but find many of the problems in Exercises¬†5.1 to be new or difficult. In MA130, there will also be an emphasis on doing problems that are closer to how you encounter mathematics beyond the classroom; your ability to take a mathematical situation and rephrase it into a formula will grow.
    On the other hand, if you are comfortable completing most of the Exercises¬†5.1 problems on your own with only minor errors, then you may be ready to enroll in MA165 Calculus or a higher-level course. Talk to your advisor and/or the mathematics program director to help you determine the right course for you. Credit for Advanced Placement exams will be considered once scores are received.
    If you are ready to confirm your mathematics course selection or inquire about courses above MA130, return to the Directed Self-Placement and check the box for the desired course of action.
